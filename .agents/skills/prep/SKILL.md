Prepare me for an interview at the company named in my message.
(If no company name is given, use the most recent folder in `applications/`.)

Read `job-analysis.md`, `resume.pdf` content, and `master-resume.tex`.
Save as `interview-prep.md` and walk me through the highlights.

1. List the 10 most likely interview questions for this role: a mix of
   role-specific, behavioral ("tell me about a time when..."), and questions
   about anything unusual on my resume (gaps, changes, short stays).
2. For each behavioral question, draft a STAR answer (Situation, Task,
   Action, Result) using ONLY real experience from the master resume.
   Mark places where I should add details from memory: [ADD DETAIL: ...].
3. For every GAP identified in the analysis, prepare one honest, confident
   way to address it if asked ("I have not used X in production, but I have
   done the related Y, and here is how I would get up to speed").
4. Suggest 5 smart questions I can ask THEM, specific to this company based
   on the research — not generic.
5. Keep the English simple and spoken-style, since I may say these answers
   out loud and English may not be my first language. Offer to run a
   practice mock interview if I want.
